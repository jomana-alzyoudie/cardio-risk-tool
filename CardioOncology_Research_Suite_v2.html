Analytical framework — PhD research context
The dose-response curve uses the Hill (Emax) sigmoidal equation: P = Emax × Dⁿ / (ED50ⁿ + Dⁿ). This is the same pharmacodynamic model applied in the MSc pre-clinical experiments (Alzyoudie, Strathclyde 2025) and proposed for population-level analysis in the PhD protocol. Sex-stratified curves demonstrate the divergent dose-response profiles hypothesised from ex vivo organ bath data.

Dexrazoxane
Iron chelator · cardioprotectant
Grade A
The only FDA-approved cardioprotectant for anthracycline cardiotoxicity. Chelates iron and prevents doxorubicin-iron complex formation, blocking ROS generation. Most evidence-based intervention available.
★★★★★Evidence
−38% CIC risk RCT data
Carvedilol / Nebivolol
Beta-blocker · antioxidant
Grade A
Carvedilol has antioxidant properties beyond beta-blockade. CECCY trial (2018): carvedilol significantly reduced troponin elevation and subclinical LVEF decline. Particularly relevant given MSc data on β-adrenergic sensitivity differences by sex.
★★★★☆Evidence
−28% LVEF decline CECCY 2018
ACE Inhibitor / ARB
Renin-angiotensin blockade
Grade A
Enalapril and candesartan: PRADA trial demonstrated candesartan significantly reduced LVEF decline vs placebo. ACE inhibitors prevent ventricular remodelling driven by neurohormonal activation after doxorubicin exposure.
★★★★☆Evidence
−24% LVEF decline PRADA trial
SGLT2 Inhibitor
Empagliflozin / Dapagliflozin
Grade B
Emerging evidence (EMPACT-MI, DAPA-HF). SGLT2i reduce cardiac inflammation and oxidative stress, improve mitochondrial function. STOP-CA trial (empagliflozin) showed significant LVEF preservation in lymphoma patients receiving anthracyclines.
★★★☆☆Evidence
−18% LVEF decline STOP-CA 2023
Statin Therapy
Rosuvastatin / Atorvastatin
Grade B
Pleiotropic effects beyond lipid-lowering: anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, endothelial protection. OVERCOME trial: rosuvastatin prevented LVEF decline and reduced troponin in haematological cancer patients. Particularly beneficial with pre-existing dyslipidaemia.
★★★☆☆Evidence
−14% LVEF decline OVERCOME trial
Liposomal Doxorubicin
Formulation modification
Grade A
Pegylated liposomal doxorubicin (Doxil/Caelyx) reduces peak cardiac drug concentration through altered biodistribution. Meta-analysis: significantly lower cardiotoxicity vs conventional doxorubicin with equivalent antitumour efficacy in eligible patients.
★★★★☆Evidence
−35% CIC incidence Meta-analysis
Continuous IV Infusion
Administration modification
Grade B
Prolonged infusion (≥6h) vs bolus: reduces peak myocardial drug concentration. Meta-analysis (van Dalen et al.): continuous infusion associated with reduced clinical heart failure. Particularly relevant for female patients given sex-specific adrenergic sensitivity data.
★★★☆☆Evidence
−22% HF incidence van Dalen 2009
Enhanced Monitoring Protocol
Serial echo + biomarkers
Grade A
ESC 2022 Cardio-Oncology guidelines: serial LVEF + GLS + hs-troponin + BNP/NT-proBNP enables early detection and prompt intervention before irreversible cardiac damage. Structured monitoring programmes reduce HF hospitalisation by enabling earlier cardioprotective initiation.
★★★★★Evidence
−30% severe CIC ESC 2022

Evidence-Based Monitoring Timeline
🔵
BEFORE TREATMENT · BASELINE
Comprehensive cardiac assessment
Echocardiogram (LVEF + GLS) · High-sensitivity troponin I/T · NT-proBNP · 12-lead ECG · Blood pressure · HbA1c (if diabetic) · Lipid profile. For female patients: document menopausal status and hormone therapy use. ESC Grade I
🟡
DURING TREATMENT · EACH CYCLE
Biomarker surveillance
hs-Troponin before each anthracycline cycle. Any new elevation ≥99th percentile → cardiology review within 2 weeks. BNP/NT-proBNP if symptomatic. Blood pressure at each visit. ESC Grade IIa
🟠
AT 200–300 mg/m² CUMULATIVE DOSE
Interim echocardiogram (high-risk patients)
For high-risk patients (score ≥40%): echocardiogram at 200–250 mg/m². Female post-menopausal patients: consider earlier threshold of 200 mg/m² based on sex-specific PD data. GLS is the most sensitive early marker — subclinical GLS decline (≥15% relative reduction) warrants intervention. Sex-specific
🔴
END OF TREATMENT
Post-treatment cardiac evaluation
Echocardiogram (LVEF + GLS) within 3 months of last dose · hs-Troponin · NT-proBNP. LVEF decline ≥10pp → initiate ACE-I + beta-blocker immediately, repeat echo in 6 weeks. ESC Grade I
🟢
6 & 12 MONTHS POST-TREATMENT
Long-term surveillance
Echocardiogram + troponin + BNP at 6 and 12 months. Low-risk patients (score <20%): single 12-month echocardiogram sufficient. High-risk: ongoing annual surveillance. Cancer survivors should enter a structured cardio-oncology follow-up programme. ESC Grade I

PhD Research Context — Monash University Application 2026
This tool is a direct proof-of-concept for the proposed PhD research: "Pharmacoepidemiology of Chemotherapy-Induced Cardiotoxicity using Real-World EHR Data and Machine Learning." All features map to specific objectives in the study protocol submitted to BMJ Open.
🧬
From Bench to Population — Research Hypothesis Map
📐 MSc Pre-clinical Data → Population-Scale PredictionsTranslational
⚡
Key translational hypothesis
MSc finding (Strathclyde 2025): Female left atrial tissue shows lower Emax and pD2 after doxorubicin → Hill equation predicts a leftward-shifted dose-response curve for females (lower ED50). Population-level EHR data (PhD Phase 2) will test whether this translates to cardiotoxicity at lower cumulative doses in female cancer patients. Machine learning models (Random Forest, XGBoost) will formally quantify the sex × dose interaction term.
🔍
Identified Research Gaps — Targets for PhD Investigation
GAP 1
No validated sex-stratified risk prediction tool exists. Current models (HFA-ICOS) treat sex as a binary categorical covariate without dose-interaction modelling. The PhD will produce the first ML-derived sex-stratified model with SHAP-explained feature importance.
GAP 2
Pre-clinical sex differences not translated to EHR populations. Multiple pre-clinical studies demonstrate sex-specific pharmacodynamics (Emax, β-adrenergic sensitivity) but no large-scale EHR analysis has tested these at population level. This is the core translational gap this research addresses.
GAP 3
Clinical informatics methodology from neurology not applied to cardio-oncology. EHR-based MS surveillance has well-validated analytical pipelines (MSBase, EDSS tracking) with no equivalent in cardio-oncology pharmacovigilance. The study protocol proposes the first formal domain transfer framework.
GAP 4
Post-menopausal cardioprotection threshold unknown. Loss of oestrogenic cardioprotection in post-menopausal patients may lower effective cardiotoxicity thresholds, but no clinical study has established sex-by-menopausal-status interaction in CIC risk. The PhD sample size plan includes adequate female patient subgroups to test this.
GAP 5
Middle Eastern / LMIC populations absent from existing models. All published cardiotoxicity prediction models were trained on Western populations. Cross-population validation using Jordanian EHR data (via current clinical informatics role) will enable generalisability analysis — a methodological contribution beyond Western contexts.
