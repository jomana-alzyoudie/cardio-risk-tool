Research context: Sex-specific risk adjustments in this tool are grounded in pre-clinical pharmacodynamic data (ex vivo organ bath, Sprague-Dawley rat model) demonstrating divergent Emax, adrenergic sensitivity, and Hill coefficient dynamics between female and male cardiac tissue following doxorubicin exposure (Alzyoudie, MSc thesis, University of Strathclyde, 2025). This tool is an experimental research prototype and does not replace clinical judgement or formal cardiac imaging.

Evidence-Based Cardioprotection Library

A research-graded catalogue of pharmacological and non-pharmacological strategies for preventing chemotherapy-induced cardiotoxicity, with sex-specific evidence highlighted. Click any strategy to see full trial data and research context.

⚡
Sex-Specific Prevention Science Grounded in pre-clinical pharmacodynamic evidence
♀ Female-Specific Strategy
Before treatment
Menopausal status assessment
Post-menopausal women lose ERα-mediated cardioprotection. Lower the effective cardiotoxicity dose threshold by ~15–20% (250 mg/m² vs 300 mg/m²). Pre-clinical basis: Alzyoudie (2025) showed doxorubicin-induced ERα downregulation disproportionately affects female cardiac tissue.
At treatment initiation
Early beta-blocker initiation
Reduced adrenergic sensitivity (lower pD2) in female cardiac tissue post-doxorubicin suggests earlier and stronger justification for carvedilol in females. CECCY trial supports troponin reduction with carvedilol; sex-stratified re-analysis is a key research gap.
Every cycle
Sex-stratified dose thresholds
Consider echocardiographic review at 200 mg/m² for females (vs 300 mg/m² standard) — supported by population data showing females reach LVEF decline threshold at ~15% lower cumulative dose (Doyle et al., JCO 2005).
Research priority
Sex-disaggregated trial analyses
Current cardioprotection trials (PRADA, CECCY, ICOS-ONE) lack powered sex-stratified subgroup analyses. The PhD protocol (Alzyoudie, 2026) will address this gap using real-world EHR data to test sex–dose interaction formally.
♂ Male-Specific Strategy
Before treatment
Topoisomerase IIβ risk context
Male cardiomyocytes express higher Topoisomerase IIβ — the molecular target of doxorubicin's direct cardiac toxicity. Greater early contractile impairment at high acute doses. Dose-fractionation strategies (infusion vs bolus) may be preferentially beneficial.
At treatment initiation
Infusion rate modification
Continuous 48–96h infusion reduces peak plasma concentration and associated acute cardiac injury. Male patients with high cumulative dose intent (>300 mg/m²) are candidates for infusion-protocol protocols. FDA label supports this approach.
Long-term
Late-onset surveillance
Male survivors may develop late-onset cardiomyopathy 10–20 years post-treatment, particularly those treated in childhood or adolescence. Annual cardiac review beyond standard 1-year follow-up is recommended for males treated at high cumulative doses.
Research priority
Mechanistic translation
The Toposiomerase IIβ pathway in male cardiac tissue remains an underexplored cardioprotection target. Bimolane and ICRF-187 (dexrazoxane analogue) mechanisms in male-specific context warrant dedicated investigation.
